I've tried and failed to start a blog so many times.

Sometimes, usually after I read a great essay on the web, I feel that I could. I could write great essays about great topics. Topics that I'm interested about, like abstraction, or symmetry. Or I could write about my hobby of annoying people by continuously asking `why`.

But I procrastinate. From the first moment that I begin my procrastination, I am fully aware that I am procrastinating. But I do it anyway. After a while, I lose the motivation to write, and I stop before I see it through.

This made me question myself. Why do I procrastinate? The very attempt at writing this entry was preceded by something more important. This paragraph is a result of several instances of procrastination, spanning at least a few weeks.

If you're always procrastinating, usually one after another, is it correct to imagine them as different instances?

Lately I've been trying to ask why a lot. I have a theory that asking why usually leads to the root of something. A nice side-effect is that it makes it possible to see, sometimes, the depth of what the other person said.

But it is a destructive process. It has the potential to destroy relationships. So I am trying to do it in moderation. But it is tempting, and really easy to get carried away, and go too far sometimes, and lose perspective.

I wanted to write more but I feel like I'm starting to procrastinate again. I should probably procrastinate procrastination. That would be useful.
